Waiver / Disclaimer / Rules

We welcome anyone interested in hiking with those hiking clubs; however, you must agree to the following and disclaimers on this page before attending any event:

As a participant, you must accept that you participate at your own risk. You must be in good physical health and with a fitness level to walk a minimum of the distance and duration described for the chosen hike.

You should come fully prepared for strenuous activity: enclosed hiking shoes, athletic / hiking pants / top, water, hat, and whatever is needed according to weather. The guides reserve the right to refuse service if you are not properly attired.

There is a greater element of risk involved in participation in the following trips. Dangers may arise including but not limited to hiking or traveling in remote areas, animals, plants, forces of nature, accident and illness. These hazards and dangers could result in personal injury, property damage, and death.

The tour guides have experience of the destinations, and their instructions must be obeyed. The tour guides, the following hiking clubs and the creator of Taiwan Hikes cannot be held responsible for any accidents or discomfort that may nevertheless occur.

Those trips often take place in areas prone to unfavourable weather conditions and may be based on logistical arrangements that are vulnerable to harsh weather, technical problems or accidents. The guides will always focus on the safety of participants on those hikes.

Any additional costs incurred in such cases, for example for new tickets, transport costs, board and lodging etc., must be covered by the participants themselves.

Those free hiking trips do not include carpooling, transportation, or transit to and from the hiking trails, and you are personally responsible for all risks associated with any travel to and from the trails.

Please carry a personal medical emergency kit and any medications you need. Always remember to let the guides know your health condition and if you are straying from the group. Stay on the trail, never walk alone and use your own common sense.

The hiking clubs are not medically trained to handle emergencies. But they will try to help you if needed. You hereby authorize any medical treatment deemed necessary in the trips of any injuries while participating in the trips.
